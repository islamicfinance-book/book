A New Kind of Contract

Bitcoin and the blockchain have kickstarted a new industry. At its core is math and cryptography which are tested, proven, and can run on any device without depending on any one app, bank, or government.

Islamic finance has developed a set of standard contracts comparable to conventional finance, especially since a resurgence in the 1970s. Regulatory approval and enthusiasm for Islamic finance varies between countries, so these banks do not cover all Muslims, nor are they limited to Muslim customers. The key distinction is a ban on interest, and investors getting their returns through equity, bonds, profit-sharing, or fees defined by contract.
